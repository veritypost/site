### 1. Migration number gap — 092 & 093 don't exist on disk [HIGH PRIORITY]

Round A/B plans say migration `092_rls_lockdown.sql` and `093_rpc_actor_lockdown.sql` were applied. Reality:

```

```

The SQL that *would* be in 092/093 lives at `99-Archive/2026-04-19-prelaunch-sprint/round_a_migration.sql` and `round_b_migration.sql`. Most likely explanation: SQL was applied directly to the live DB via MCP `execute_sql`, but the numbered files were never committed to `01-Schema/`.

**Consequence:** `01-Schema/reset_and_rebuild_v2.sql` + numbered migrations are supposed to be a faithful disaster-recovery replay of live DB. If 092/093 never landed on disk, a cold-start rebuild will miss the Round A RLS lockdown + Round B RPC actor-spoof fixes. That's the single largest security delta from yesterday.

**Owner question:** Were Round A/B SQL files intentionally kept out of `01-Schema/`, or is this an oversight? If oversight, copy `round_a_migration.sql` → `01-Schema/092_*.sql` and `round_b_migration.sql` → `01-Schema/093_*.sql`.
