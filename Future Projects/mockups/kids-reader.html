Scientists found a way to recycle plastic using only sunlight

A team in Australia made a special powder that can break plastic bottles into smaller parts when the sun hits them. If it works at bigger scale, it could make recycling a lot cheaper — and mean a lot less plastic trash in oceans and on beaches.

The team is at the University of New South Wales, in Sydney. They mixed a kind of chemical powder with water and then put plastic bottles into the mixture. When sunlight shone on it, the plastic started to break down into little building blocks.

Those building blocks can be used to make new plastic. Normally, recycling plastic uses a lot of heat, which costs money and uses a lot of energy. The sunlight method might use way less energy — maybe 40% less, the scientists think.

It's still a lab experiment. The scientists say it could take five or six years before big recycling centers could use it, because they need to test it at a much bigger size and make sure it's safe and works on lots of different kinds of plastic.

Kids in Sydney can walk past the lab where this is happening. The university said anyone can visit on weekends.
