Law · Supreme Court

Supreme Court hears TikTok divestment challenge; ruling expected before January 19 deadline

The 2-hour, 40-minute argument concerned the April 2024 law requiring ByteDance to sell TikTok's U.S. operations or face a ban.

The U.S. Supreme Court heard 2 hours and 40 minutes of oral argument on Friday in TikTok Inc. v. Garland, the challenge to the law requiring ByteDance to divest TikTok's U.S. operations or face a ban by January 19. The D.C. Circuit upheld the law unanimously on December 6. TikTok has approximately 170 million U.S. users; if the Court does not stay or reverse the D.C. Circuit ruling, federal prohibition takes effect unless a qualifying divestment is completed.

Fed cuts rates 25 basis points in 11-1 vote; Hammack dissents citing inflation

The Federal Reserve lowered the federal funds rate to a 4.00–4.25% range, its second cut of the year. Cleveland Fed President Beth Hammack cast the sole dissenting vote, saying the inflation trajectory did not yet justify easing.

A team at the University of New South Wales reported a catalyst that breaks PET bottles into reusable monomers using only sunlight. If manufactured at scale, the team estimates the process could reduce recycling energy costs by 40% compared with current thermal methods.
