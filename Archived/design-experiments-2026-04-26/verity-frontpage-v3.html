Law · Constitutional

Supreme Court overturns Chevron doctrine in 6-3 ruling, ending 40 years of agency deference

The decision reshapes the balance of power between Congress, the courts, and federal agencies — and immediately threatens pending EPA, SEC, and FTC regulations.

The U.S. Supreme Court ruled 6-3 on Wednesday in Loper Bright Enterprises v. Raimondo that federal agencies do not have inherent authority to interpret ambiguous statutes in their favor. Chief Justice Roberts, writing for the majority, held that courts must exercise independent judgment in determining the meaning of statutory provisions rather than deferring to agency interpretations. The ruling overturns Chevron U.S.A. v. Natural Resources Defense Council, the 1984 precedent that had become one of the most-cited cases in administrative law.
