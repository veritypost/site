Supreme Court hears TikTok divestment challenge; ruling expected before January 19 deadline

The 2-hour, 40-minute argument concerned the April 2024 law requiring ByteDance to sell TikTok's U.S. operations or face a ban.

The U.S. Supreme Court on Friday heard oral argument in TikTok Inc. v. Garland, a challenge to the federal law requiring ByteDance to divest TikTok's United States operations or face prohibition. The argument ran 2 hours and 40 minutes, nine days before the law's effective date.

The law, the Protecting Americans from Foreign Adversary Controlled Applications Act, passed the House 352-65 last March and the Senate 79-18 in April. President Biden signed it the following day. The statute gives the executive branch authority to prohibit distribution of any mobile application controlled by a designated foreign adversary.

Solicitor General Elizabeth Prelogar argued for the government. Prelogar said the law addresses "the national security threat posed by the PRC's ability to demand access to data on 170 million Americans and to manipulate the content those Americans see."
